# Jaaga Game Rules

Jaaga Game is a 2-6 player card game played over multiple hands. The goal is to finish the game with the fewest total points.

## Deck

Use one standard 52-card deck plus 2 Jokers, for a total of 54 cards.

## Card Values

Card values are used when a hand ends and players score the cards left in their hands.

| Card | Points |
| --- | ---: |
| Ace | 1 |
| 2-10 | Face value |
| Jack, Queen, King | 10 |
| Joker, for the player who goes out | 1 |
| Joker, for all other players | 40 |

## Setup

Choose the first dealer randomly. For later hands, the winner of the previous hand becomes the dealer.

The dealer shuffles the full deck and deals 5 cards to each player. The remaining cards become the draw pile. Turn one card from the draw pile face-up to start the discard pile.

Play proceeds clockwise, beginning with the player to the dealer's left.

## Objective

The game is played in hands. At the end of each hand, players score points for the cards remaining in their hands. Lower scores are better.

The game ends after any hand where at least one player has a total score of 100 points or more. The player with the lowest total score wins the game.

## Turn Overview

At the beginning of their turn, a player may go out if their hand is worth 5 points or less. If they go out, the hand ends immediately.

If the player does not go out, they take a normal turn:

1. Discard one legal discard set.
2. Draw one card.

After drawing, that player's turn ends and play immediately moves to the next player. There is no pass action.

## Legal Discards

A player must discard before drawing. A legal discard is one of the following:

- Any single card.
- Any number of cards with the same rank, such as three 2s or two Kings.
- A same-suit straight of at least 3 cards.

Jokers are wild during play. A Joker may represent any card needed to complete a same-rank set or same-suit straight.

## Same-Suit Straights

A same-suit straight is a sequence of at least 3 cards in rank order, all in the same suit.

Aces may be low or high:

- `A-2-3` is valid.
- `Q-K-A` is valid.
- `K-A-2` is not valid.

## Drawing

After discarding, the player draws exactly one card from either:

- The top of the draw pile.
- Any one card from the immediately preceding player's last discard set.

The preceding player's last discard set means the card or cards discarded by the previous player on their most recent turn.

## Slap

After drawing, if the drawn card could have been included in the discard set the player just made, the player may slap. A player cannot slap a Joker.

A successful slap immediately adds the drawn card to that discard set. The player must slap before the next player discards. Once the next player discards, the slap window closes.

Examples:

- A player discards two 7s, then draws another 7. They may slap and add the drawn 7 to that discard set.
- A player discards `4-5-6` of hearts, then draws the 7 of hearts. They may slap and add the 7 of hearts to that discard set.
- A player discards a single Queen, then draws another Queen. They may slap and add the drawn Queen to that discard set.

## Going Out

A player may go out only at the beginning of their own turn and only if the cards in their hand are worth 5 points or less.

When a player goes out, the hand ends immediately and all players reveal their hands.

If no other player has the same number of points or fewer, the player who went out scores 0 points. The cards remaining in their hand are disregarded.

If any other player has the same number of points or fewer, the player who went out scores 30 points. The cards remaining in their hand are still disregarded.

## End of Hand

When a hand ends, each player scores the cards remaining in their hand using the card values table.

The winner of the hand is the player with the lowest scored hand after any penalty is applied.

If multiple players tie for the lowest hand score, break the tie by turn order starting from the player who went out.

The winner of the hand becomes the dealer for the next hand.

## Empty Draw Pile

If the draw pile runs out during a hand, keep the latest discard set available. Shuffle the older discarded cards to create a new draw pile.

If there are no older discarded cards available to shuffle, players continue drawing from the preceding player's last discard set when possible until the hand ends or a draw pile can be formed.
